Education authorities are facing a serious integrity challenge. The Department of Basic Education has confirmed a breach in the marking process for the two thousand and twenty five National Senior Certificate examinations. Early reports indicate the breach was discovered during marking procedures and has been escalated to Umalusi, the quality assurance body. Coverage across outlets notes the department is cooperating with oversight bodies and that investigations are under way to assess the scope of the problem and any impact on results. This development will be closely watched by parents, schools and learners as officials determine next steps and possible remedies. Education unions have called for full transparency to protect the credibility of the exams and to ensure that no learner is unfairly disadvantaged.

Two new nature reserves were declared and two existing reserves were extended, creating vital space for the Cape mountain zebra to breed and strengthening protections for threatened succulent plants, mammals and bird species across the province. Conservation groups and provincial authorities say the expansion will help biodiversity and ecosystem resilience in a region facing ongoing environmental pressures. Officials say the move also supports local tourism and community benefits linked to conservation stewardship.
