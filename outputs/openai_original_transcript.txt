Gauteng education officials say online placement for grades one and eight has largely been completed, yet thousands remain unplaced and transport challenges persist across provinces. The implications are immediate for attendance, safety and schooling outcomes as the new academic year approaches.

A controversy in the Western Cape is drawing national attention. Bitou deputy mayor Nokuzola Kolwapi has denied discharging a firearm during her son’s umgidi initiation ceremony in KwaNokuthula, Plettenberg Bay. Viral video footage showed what appeared to be a person firing into the air at a traditional event. Kolwapi maintains that the object she held was a replica toy firearm and not a real weapon. The incident has prompted charges to be laid and an investigation to be opened, while political parties say they will wait for the outcome of the police probe before commenting further. The incident has sparked a wider debate about public conduct during traditional ceremonies and the responsibilities of public office-bearers.
